A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man Of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and passed away on July 27, 2015.

Biography

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • Studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • Joined ISRO in 1969 and played a pivotal role in India's space program.
  • Contributed to the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle.
  • Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister from 1992 to 1999.
  • Played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Elected as the 11th President of India in 2002 and served until 2007.
  • Known as the "Missile Man Of India" for his work in missile development.
